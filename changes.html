Changes in new call report (PCR) program

  • Patient information (name, DOB, address) is stored separately from the call report. This allows quicker call entry (just search for a name or address) and also allows us to keep statistics on how often our patients are transported. To input patient information, click on "Find Patient" and first search for a patient. It's probably best to just enter the first few letters of their last name, or the street they live on. If you don't get any results, click on "Add New Patient" and enter the information. The patients from the past few years have already been added, if they had complete information.
  • Call Locations are stored separately as well, and in a structured format. Click on the "Call Location" box, you can search for previous locations (i.e. type in Parisis and it will come back with the right address) or enter a new one as either a street address or an intersection.
  • By default, only one row of vitals is shown. Click "Add another set of vitals" to add more rows as needed.
  • By default, three crew members are shown. Click "Add Crew Member" to add crew members as needed.
  • You now only need to enter each member's ID once. Just check the boxes for who was the driver.
  • In the crew area, BEFORE YOU ENTER ANYONE'S NUMBER, click on "Get Duty Crew". The duty crew at the time of dispatch will automatically be filled in.
  • Crew member Duty/General status is stored with the call report at the time it was submitted. Whether the call was a Duty or General call is automatically calculated.
