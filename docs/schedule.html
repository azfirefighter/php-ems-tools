schedule.php

The schedule layout is fairly straightforward. It displays a monthly schedule, showing all of the days in a given month with six slots per day. In custom.php, you can restrict sign-ons for shifts previous to the current one (allowing only administrators to change events in the past) as well as optionally requiring a password or administrator for sign-ons, removals, or changes. The "<< Month" link goes to the previous month's schedule, and the " Month >>" link goes to the next month. The "HELP" link opens a JavaScript Pop-Up with some simple instructions and explanations. When viewing the Night schedule, the "Days" link shows the day schedule for the current month. The "Nights" link on the Days schedule works likewise. The "Count Hours" link goes directly to the Count Monthly Hours page for the month being viewed. The "Current Shift" link goes to the current shift (by month and time of day).

If the current date is the 15th of the month or later, the schedule automatically scrolls down to the lower half of the page, if the current month is being viewed.

The schedule page automatically refreshes itself every time a change is made by the computer that is viewing it. It also refreshes itself every 180 seconds (3 minutes) to update changes that may have been made on different computers.

To sign a member on, simply click in an empty slot (dashed box) in the desired day of the desired month/shift. This will open a JavaScript window to sign on. If the settings in custom.php dictate it, an ID and password may need to be entered. The member can select the action to be taken (Sign On, Edit - to change ID or times, or Remove), type in their ID, select the start and end times (by default, set to the start and end of the shift), and click Submit. The pop-up will submit the data, refresh the schedule window, and close itself.

To set a message for a particular day (text to display next to the date), click in the area next to the date. A similar pop-up will open, allowing the administrator to enter their ID and password, and add/edit or remove a message. It is recommended that this task be restricted to administrators/officers, but this can be changed in custom.php

Email & text message alerts:
The schedule has the option to send out email alerts and/or text messages (via email) to the other members in a given shift to notify them of a member signing on, editing their times, or signing off. These options are set in the custom.php file. Currently, the only way to disable alerts for a specific member is to remove their email or textEmail address from the roster.
